Fixtures vs Traces — Example Inventory

Does this separation feel right? Are there items that don't fit cleanly?

Fixtures (what a place is)

Defined by address/location templates. Always present. Not serialized in saves.

Suburban Home
  • Computer in office/bedroom
  • Landline phone in kitchen
  • TV in living room
  • Mailbox at front door
  • Answering machine
  • Medicine cabinet in bathroom
  • Trash cans (kitchen, exterior)
  • Filing cabinet in office
  • Garage (car inside if owner has one)
  • Backyard fence/gate
Apartment Unit
  • Computer in office/bedroom
  • Landline phone
  • Intercom/buzzer system
  • Balcony (if present)
  • Trash chute access
  • Fire escape window
  • Medicine cabinet
Apartment Building (shared)
  • Mailbox bank in lobby
  • Security camera system (in security room)
  • Elevator
  • Dumpster in exterior lot
  • Visitor sign-in log
  • Fire alarm system
  • Intercom panel at entrance
Diner / Bar
  • Cash register
  • Payphone
  • Jukebox
  • Kitchen equipment
  • Walk-in cooler/freezer
  • Manager's desk & safe
  • Dumpster out back
  • Restroom
  • Booth seating / bar stools
Office Building
  • Receptionist desk
  • Filing cabinets
  • Computers at desks
  • Copy machine
  • Manager's office (with safe?)
  • Break room vending machines
  • Parking garage
  • Security desk in lobby
Park
  • Park benches
  • Trash cans
  • Payphone
  • Public restroom
  • Pond / fountain
  • Wooded area (concealment)
  • Jogging path
  • Parking lot

Traces (what happened)

Generated by NPC actions, events, and facts. Serialized in saves. Can decay.

Residency (fact-based, bulk generation)
  • Resident's fingerprints throughout home
  • Personal items (clothing, photos, books)
  • Mail addressed to resident
  • Prescription bottles with name
  • Personal diary / journal
  • Car in garage/lot matching resident
  • Name on mailbox / door
  • Bills and bank statements
Daily routine (action-based, decaying)
  • Dirty dishes in sink (was home recently)
  • Warm coffee pot
  • Newspaper on doorstep (or not — picked up?)
  • Bed unmade / made
  • Lights left on
  • Cigarette butts in ashtray
  • Receipts in trash
  • Food in fridge (fresh vs expired)
  • Answering machine messages
Social visits (action-based, decaying)
  • Visitor's fingerprints (scattered rooms)
  • Extra wine glass / coffee mug
  • Lipstick on glass
  • Unfamiliar coat on hook
  • Perfume/cologne lingering
  • Two sets of dishes
  • Guest's forgotten item (earring, lighter)
Crime — violent break-in
  • Broken window / smashed door
  • Killer's fingerprints (window, walls, handles)
  • Blood trail (between rooms)
  • Blood pools (fight locations)
  • Bloody handprint on door handle
  • Signs of struggle (smashed furniture)
  • Corpse (location, position, wounds)
  • Murder weapon (present or missing)
  • Victim's defensive wounds
  • Killer's blood (if injured)
  • Torn clothing fibers
  • Footprints (mud, blood)
  • Neighbor witness reports (heard glass, screaming)
Crime — burglary
  • Picked lock (scratch marks)
  • Missing items (safe emptied, jewelry gone)
  • Burglar's fingerprints
  • Drawers ransacked
  • Disabled alarm / cut wires
  • Footprints at entry point
  • Dropped tool (lockpick, crowbar)
Crime — meeting / conspiracy
  • Multiple people's fingerprints in one room
  • Cigarette butts (multiple brands)
  • Notes / maps left behind
  • Overheard conversation (witness)
  • Unfamiliar cars in lot during odd hours
  • Cash or contraband stashed
Workplace activity
  • Employee's fingerprints at their station
  • Sign-in/sign-out logs
  • Security camera footage (if accessible)
  • Personal items at desk
  • Coworker witness sightings
Public spaces
  • Fingerprints on payphone
  • Discarded items in trash can
  • Witness sightings (someone loitering)
  • Parked car at unusual hour
  • Dropped item on ground

Gray Area Items — Where Do These Belong?

Some things could arguably be either. Worth discussing.

  • A person's car parked at home — fixture of the person? Or a trace of "person is home"?
  • Family photos on the wall — fixture of the home template? Or trace of residency?
  • Food in the fridge — fixture (every home has food)? Or trace (decays, indicates recent activity)?
  • A hidden safe behind a painting — fixture of the home? Or a discoverable secret?
  • Stashed contraband — placed by a crime action (trace), but persists indefinitely like a fixture
  • Security camera footage — the camera is a fixture, but the footage is a trace of events
