A Day in the Life: Office Worker "Dave"

Same person, same day — comparing sublocation assignment on arrival vs. intra-location movement. Note the traces each approach naturally produces.

A) Assign on Arrival

NPC teleports to a sublocation per activity. Traces generated by a "trace factory" attached to the action.

6:00 AM — Home (Apartment 4B)
Action: Sleep → Wake Up
Assigned to: Bedroom
Traces (backfilled):
· Fingerprints on bedroom door ✓
· Fingerprints on bathroom door ← how? never "went" there
· Fingerprints on front door ← generated at departure?
· Coffee mug in kitchen ← never "went" to kitchen
7:00 AM — Travel by Car
→ Office at 456 Fantasy Lane
7:30 AM — Office (456 Fantasy Lane)
Action: Work (7:30 AM - 12:00 PM)
Assigned to: Cubicle Area
Traces (backfilled for 4.5hr work block):
· Fingerprints on lobby door ✓
· Fingerprints on elevator buttons ← never "rode" elevator
· Security camera log in lobby ← never "passed through" lobby
· Fingerprints on break room coffee pot ← never "visited" break room
· Bathroom usage ← never "went" to bathroom
· When did each trace happen? Timestamps are invented.

Assigned to: Living Room → Bedroom

· Lock front door? When? Was it already locked?
· TV remote fingerprints ← in living room but assigned to bedroom?
Problems with this approach:
  • Traces in rooms the NPC never "visited" feel fabricated
  • Timestamps on backfilled traces are invented, not simulated
  • Trace factory must know every possible sublocation interaction per action type — combinatorial explosion
  • Can't answer "was Dave in the break room at 10 AM?" truthfully
  • Two parallel systems: the real simulation + the trace fiction

B) Intra-location Movement

NPC moves through sublocations as sub-actions. Traces are natural byproducts of movement.

6:00 AM — Home (Apartment 4B)
Sub-actions:
6:00 — Wake up in Bedroom
6:05 — Walk to Bathroom (shower)
6:25 — Walk to Kitchen (make coffee)
6:45 — Walk to Living Room (eat breakfast)
7:00 — Walk to Front Door (leave)
Traces (natural byproducts):
· 6:05 Fingerprints on bathroom door ✓
· 6:25 Fingerprints on kitchen cabinets ✓
· 6:25 Coffee mug left in kitchen sink ✓
· 7:00 Fingerprints on front door ✓
· 7:00 Front door locked (key used) ✓
Every trace has a real timestamp and real location.
7:00 AM — Travel by Car
→ Office at 456 Fantasy Lane
7:30 AM — Office (456 Fantasy Lane)
Sub-actions:
7:30 — Enter Lobby (badge in)
7:32 — Elevator → Floor 3
7:35 — Arrive at Cubicle Area
9:45 — Walk to Break Room (coffee)
9:55 — Return to Cubicle Area
11:00 — Walk to Restroom
11:05 — Return to Cubicle Area
12:00 — Elevator → Lobby (leave for lunch)
Traces (natural byproducts):
· 7:30 Security cam captures lobby entry ✓
· 7:32 Fingerprint on elevator button (Floor 3) ✓
· 9:45 Fingerprints on coffee pot in break room ✓
· 9:45 Coffee grounds in break room trash ✓
· 12:00 Security cam captures lobby exit ✓
Can answer: "Was Dave in the break room at 10 AM?" → No, he left at 9:55.

8:00 — Front Door (unlock, enter)

10:30 — Bathroom (brush teeth)

Traces:
· 8:00 Fingerprints on front door, key ✓
· 8:00 Front door unlocked (forgot to lock!) ✓
· 8:02 Dirty plate in kitchen ✓
· 8:15 TV remote fingerprints ✓
The unlocked door is why the killer gets in at 2 AM.
Why this works:
  • Traces are real — every one has a true timestamp and location
  • One system, not two — no trace factory, no backfill
  • "Was X at Y at time Z?" is answerable from simulation history
  • Emergent stories: the forgotten door lock leads to a murder
  • Sub-actions are simple: move to room + do thing + leave trace
  • Investigation mechanics can trust the simulation completely
