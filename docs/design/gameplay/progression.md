The player starts as a lowly gumshoe with almost no money to their name.

They start taking incredibly low-stakes "cases" - picking up jobs off local bulletin boards in bars, until they can afford to take out an ad in the yellow pages. As they get some word of mouth, they get calls for slightly more involved cases (I think my husband is cheating on me, I want incriminating photos), that pay more.

Eventually they solve a murder case or two even though the police don't want their help. It catches the police chief's attention, who calls the PI for help on a tough case. Now the player is working with the police, instead of trying to avoid them.

Eventually, the player bumps into a case that is a lot bigger - perhaps they stumble on a murder that is related to drug cartels. The player gets an offer from a shady figure asking for help with the case, and providing some intel. If the player does well, they are given an opportunity to work with the CIA on bigger cases like that more frequently.

If they do well on those, they get recruited into the CIA as a spy. Now they are working on huge cases, like national security concerns and international terrorist plots.

Personal problems → Local crime → Organized crime → Federal crime → International espionage
Each tier introduces new mechanics, new investigative tools, and new moral ambiguity.

Below is a structured escalation ladder you can mine for missions.

🕵️ Tier 1 — Gumshoe Jobs (Barely Crimes)

Theme: Survival, humiliation, learning the basics
Pay: Almost nothing
Sources: Bar bulletin boards, word of mouth
Skills taught: Observation, tailing, photography, dialogue

These feel small, messy, and human.

Everyday “Cases”
Missing pet (actually stolen by neighbor)
Recover stolen bicycle
Track down someone who skipped rent
Deliver a suspicious package (player realizes it's shady)
Find runaway teenager
Verify alibi for bar fight
Photograph cheating spouse
Background check on new boyfriend/girlfriend
Catch employee faking injury
Find lost heirloom
Minor Crimes Discovered Along the Way
Shoplifting ring
Insurance fraud
Small-time scams
Fake charity collections
Illegal gambling in back rooms

👉 Player learns: people lie constantly.

🚔 Tier 2 — Private Investigator Work

Theme: Real consequences begin
Pay: Enough to survive + Yellow Pages ad unlocked
Sources: Phone calls, lawyers, small businesses

Now investigations require planning and evidence.

Domestic & Civil Cases
Divorce evidence gathering
Custody disputes
Blackmail investigation
Corporate employee theft
Stalker identification
Missing persons (voluntary disappearance vs foul play)
Criminal Overlap
Burglary patterns
Car theft chop shops
Loan shark intimidation
Arson-for-insurance schemes
Identity fraud (80s-style paper identity theft)

👉 First hint: crimes connect to recurring NPCs.

Theme: You’re in over your head
Police: Suspicious or hostile

The player starts encountering violence.

Escalating Crimes

Armed robbery investigations
Witness intimidation
Drug dealing at clubs
Corrupt landlord schemes
Union intimidation
Prostitution rings
First Major Turning Point
Player solves a murder police mishandled
Evidence chain mechanic introduced
Police chief notices competence

🔺 Escalation Pattern (Design Secret)

Here’s the hidden structure that makes progression feel natural:

Stage	Question Player Asks
Gumshoe	“What happened?”
PI	“Who’s lying?”
Police	“Who benefits?”
Organized Crime	“Who’s in charge?”
Intelligence	“Who’s pulling strings?”
CIA	“What’s the real objective?”
